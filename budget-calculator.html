Estimate the general level of performance you can expect from your budget:

*Result is a very generalized assumption to provide an idea of what to expect when in the market for a PC.

Low Budget

<$249

You'll be hard pressed to find a computer that runs a modern version of windows and would serve much use at this price range. There are other options like Chromebooks, which are great if you only need to use a browser and some plugins. There are some possibilities for very basic use computer if using a Linux distribution, such as Ubuntu, is an option for the user.

For less than $250, there really aren't that many options unless some parts are being taken from other computers already owned.

Basic Needs

$250-$449

At this price range, you can get a computer that will handle browsing the internet and editing documents without issue. There may be occasional slow-downs when running a few windows of browser tabs and documents, but keeping the multitasking to a minimum will resolve the issue.

Entry Level

$450-$699

At this price range, you can get a computer that will be much more capable of running multiple windows/programs at the same time. The budget would get a computer that is capable of maxing out simple/older games and be able to play some of the more intensive AAA titles at low or medium settings.

Mid-tier

$700-$899

This budget allows for a mid-range gaming PC that will be able to run a couple of monitors easily and handle multitasking of browser tabs and documents like it's nothing. Given a better Dedicated GPU, a PC in this range can play newer AAA titles at medium-high settings.

High end

$900-$1199

This budget allows for a high-end gaming PC that can handle any of the newest titles at high-ultra settings at 1080p or 1440p easily. Rendering 1080p videos in editing software from the likes of Adobe will have no issue scrubbing through footage.

Extreme end

$1200+

This budget is for enthusiasts who want the absolute most out of their PC. Whether its playing games at 4k resolution or rendering 4k footage in Adobe, the user will no doubt have a good time and can expect the PC to last them a while.
